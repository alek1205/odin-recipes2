Beef Ramen

This simple, warming Spicy Miso Braised Beef Ramen is just the right bowl you need to warm up with.

Slow-cooked beef served in a steaming rich French-onion-style broth with miso, chilies, and Chinese 5 spice for added flavor and spice.

It’s just perfectly spicy (spice to your liking) and filled with plenty of yummy ramen noodles.

It’s different, but one of the best bowls of homemade ramen!

Ingredients

  • 1(2 pound) beef chunk roast
  • black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons salted butter
  • 3 yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 shallots,halved
  • 2 cups dry white wine
  • 3-4 cups beef or chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup tamari or soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 1-2 tablespoons chili flakes,to your taste
  • 1 tablespoon Chinese 5 spice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh ginger
  • 4 squares ramen noodles
  • soft or hard boiled eggs
  • toasted sesame seeds, green onions, and chili flakes

Step's

  1. season the beef

    I used a beef pot roast, which was great since we’re slow-cooking the meat. It always turns out tender this way.

    Season the meat with black pepper.

  2. the onions

    Now that you took care of the roast, start the onions. The key here is to slice them and use A LOT.

    Toss the onions in a large Dutch oven that you can transfer to the oven. Let them cook along with the shallots in a little butter. You just want to get the cooking started but don’t fully cook the onions.

  3. braise

    The rest of this process is so easy. Put the roast on top of the onions. Add the remaining wine, broth, tamari (or use soy), white miso paste, chili flakes, Chinese 5 spice, and fresh ginger.

  4. get the meat caramelized

    Once the meat has cooked, you can remove the lid and turn the heat up in the oven. Continue to cook until the meat gets some caramelization and color on top.

    Then, shred the meat and add the remaining broth.

  5. serve

    Cook the noodles in boiling water, then arrange them in bowls. Ladle the broth and beef over the noodles.

    It’s a little spicy but the flavor is spot-on, and the caramelized onions make for a rich, decadent bowl!
